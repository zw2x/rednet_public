# H-Dimer Design Analysis

In one-sided interface fixed-backbone sequence design, input consists of all-atom coordinates for the bound target and backbone coordinates (C, CA, N, O) for the binder. Binder sequences are predicted and evaluated via self-consistency tests using AlphaFold3. Self-consistency assesses whether the designed sequence, when folded by a structure prediction oracle, adopts the intended target structure. This involves comparing the predicted structure against the input backbone using metrics like RMSD and TM-score, alongside model confidence scores like pLDDT and iPTM. We refrain from providing MSA to AlphaFold3 to prevent artificial confidence inflation from evolutionary information, relying instead on the target templates.

We benchmark RedNet against ESM-IF, ProteinMPNN, PiFold, and SolMPNN on heterodimer design tasks. We evaluate performance across two distinct datasets: all 107 heterodimeric targets (*Table 1*) and a high-confidence subset of 44 targets (*Table 2*). We analyze two primary failure modes: Type-1 consistency (monomer self-consistency, metrics: `dsn_chain_plddt`) and Type-2 consistency (heterodimer self-consistency, metrics: `success_rate`, `ptm`, and `iptm`).

### All Targets

On the full dataset of 107 heterodimers, all methods face significant challenges. Native sequences achieve a success rate of only 29%, with structure prediction metrics indicating difficulty in modeling these complexes (mean `iptm` = 0.39). ProteinMPNN (n030) demonstrates strong monomer self-consistency (Type-1), achieving the highest design chain confidence (`dsn_chain_plddt` = 56.59) compared to native sequences (51.31). SolMPNN (chain pLDDT = 54.79), ESM-IF (chain pLDDT = 53.77), and RedNet (chain pLDDT = 53.12 for b09) remain competitive and perform above native sequences; only PiFold (50.29) underperforms. However, improved monomer self-consistency does not translate to superior heterodimer self-consistency (Type-2): ProteinMPNN (n030) achieves a success rate of 30%, comparable to RedNet's best contrastive decoding model (30%). The low baseline performance suggests that AlphaFold3's predictive accuracy on this full dataset is limited when restricted to single sequences and templates.

### High-Quality Targets

To disentangle design capability from prediction error, we analyze a subset of 44 complexes where native sequences yield high-confidence structure predictions (pLDDT > 70) for target chains. In this regime, performance stratifications become distinct. RedNet with contrastive decoding achieves a success rate of 68%, outperforming all other methods. Specifically, RedNet surpasses SolMPNN (66%), ProteinMPNN (66%), PiFold (64%), and ESM-IF (61%).

**Effect of Contrastive Decoding.** Contrastive decoding significantly enhances RedNet's ability to improve Type-2 consistency. The baseline RedNet model (t0001) achieves a success rate of 57% on the high-quality subset. The introduction of contrastive decoding (model b09) increases the success rate to 68%, an absolute improvement of 11 percentage points. This gain indicates that contrastive decoding effectively steers the generative process toward sequences that not only adopt the correct fold but also form high-confidence interfaces (`iptm` = 0.57 vs 0.56 for baseline). One limitation of contrastive decoding is that it degrades monomer self-consistency at lower beta thresholds (e.g., `dsn_chain_plddt` of 66.18 for b03 vs 67.71 for b09). This occurs because the contrastive objective penalizes regions where the sequence probabilities of the unbound monomer are high, potentially disrupting internal folding consistency.

**Robustness and Comparison.** While ProteinMPNN consistently produces sequences with higher predicted monomer confidence (mean `dsn_chain_plddt` = 70.41 for n010 vs 67.71 for RedNet b09), RedNet demonstrates superior complex success rates (68% vs 66%). Interestingly, RedNet achieves this higher success rate despite lower mean metric scores (e.g., mean `iptm` 0.57 vs 0.61 for ProteinMPNN). This discrepancy suggests that ProteinMPNN's failures may be more severe or that RedNet's designs are more consistently "good enough" to pass the strict multi-objective thresholds for success (pLDDT > 80, pTM > 0.55, iPTM > 0.5). It implies that while signal from backbone coordinates alone (ProteinMPNN) is sufficient for maximizing general fold confidence, the side-chain context utilized by RedNet provides critical, specific information for optimizing interface interactions, thereby achieving native-level heterodimer self-consistency.
